# DATATHON FINAL ROUND

- **Data Analytics Challenge: Customer Intelligence, Revenue Prediction & Interactive Dashboard Insights**

- **Business Background**
  - Following the dashboard stage, your team has already explored customer behavior, revenue streams, and customer experience patterns for the live entertainment venue.
  - Now, venue management wants to move beyond descriptive analytics and use **Machine Learning (ML) and Advanced Analytics** to uncover deeper patterns, predict future outcomes, and support strategic business decisions.
  - Management is no longer asking “What happened?” — they want to know:
    1. Which customers are most likely to become high value repeat visitors ?
    2. Which customer segments are at risk of low satisfaction or poor recommendation?
    3. Can future revenue be predicted based on customer profile and behavior?
    4. What hidden customer groups exist beyond visible demographics?
    5. Which operational or marketing actions could increase profitability and customer loyalty?
  - Your role in the final stage is to act as a **Data Science & Strategy Consulting Team** and develop ML-powered solutions that transform the same dataset into predictive and strategic intelligence.

- **Final Presentation Guidelines**
  - Following the Dashboard Competition stage, shortlisted teams are required to present their previously developed dashboard together with an extended analytical solution incorporating Machine Learning (ML) techniques and strategic business insights.
  - Your solution must go beyond dashboarding and focus on **business problem solving through ML-driven analysis.**

- **Presentation Expectations**
  - Each team presentation should include:

  **1. Dashboard Overview**
  - Teams must briefly present:
    - The dashboard developed during the Dashboard Competition
    - Key insights identified
    - Business understanding gained from the dataset
    - Important visualizations and storytelling elements

  **2. Technology & Tools Used**
  - Teams should clearly explain:
    - Technologies/platforms used
    - Software/tools utilized
    - Libraries/packages/frameworks used (if applicable)
    - Examples:
      - Power BI
      - Tableau
      - Python
      - R
      - Streamlit
      - Shiny
      - Excel

  **3. Machine Learning / Advanced Analytics Component**
  - Teams are expected to incorporate at least one predictive or advanced analytical component into their solution.
  - Possible examples include:
    - Classification models
    - Regression models
    - Clustering/segmentation
    - Recommendation systems
    - Trend forecasting
    - Customer segmentation
  - Teams are encouraged to focus more on:
    - Business relevance
    - Interpretation
    - Practical usefulness rather than highly complex modeling

**Core Objective**

- Develop data-driven ML solutions that help management:
  - **Predictive Goals**
    - Predict which customers are likely to become repeat visitors
    - Predict customer satisfaction and recommendation likelihood
    - Forecast customer revenue potential
    - Identify drivers of customer retention and profitability
  - **Strategic Goals**
    - Segment customers using data-driven clustering
    - Identify high-value and low-value customer profiles
    - Recommend strategies to improve customer experience
    - Suggest business interventions for revenue growth

  **4. Business Insights & Recommendations**
  - Teams should explain:
    - Key findings
    - Strategic recommendations
    - Business decisions supported by the analysis
    - Potential improvements for customer engagement, revenue, or operational performance

- **Technical Expectations**
  - Teams are expected to demonstrate:
    - Proper data understanding
    - Basic preprocessing/data preparation
    - Appropriate model selection
    - Clear interpretation of outputs
    - Effective visualization and communication

- **Final Deliverables**
  - Each team must submit:
    1. Presentation Deck (15 Minutes)
       - Must cover:
         - Business understanding
         - Data preparation
         - ML methodology
         - Key insights
         - Strategic recommendations
         - Visual storytelling
    2. Technical Documentation
       - Code / Notebook
       - Model summary
       - Assumptions
       - Evaluation results
    3. Viva (10 Minutes)
       - Teams should be prepared to defend:
         - Why specific models were chosen
         - Data preparation logic
         - Feature engineering choices
         - Business relevance
         - Limitations

- **Important Note**
  - The final round evaluation will place significant emphasis on:
    - Visualization quality
    - Storytelling ability
    - Business understanding
    - Explainability of the solution
    - Practical application of analytics and ML techniques
